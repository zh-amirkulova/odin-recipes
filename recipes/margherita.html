Description

Pizza Margherita is a classic Italian dish known for its simplicity and fresh ingredients. It features a thin, crispy crust topped with a rich tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and vibrant basil leaves. The pizza is typically baked until the crust is golden and the cheese melts into a gooey, delicious layer. A drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of salt add the finishing touches, enhancing the natural flavors. This pizza is light, flavorful, and embodies the essence of Italian cuisine, showcasing the harmonious combination of just a few high-quality ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Tomato sauce (about 1/2 cup)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese (200g, sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves (a handful)
  • Olive oil (1-2 tablespoons)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Optional: Grated Parmesan cheese (for extra flavor)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven:
    • Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it heats up.
  2. Prepare the Dough:
    • If using store-bought dough, roll it out on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
    • If making dough from scratch, prepare it ahead of time and allow it to rise.
  3. Add the Sauce:
    • Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving about a 1-inch border for the crust.
  4. Add Cheese:
    • Distribute the sliced mozzarella evenly over the tomato sauce.
  5. Bake:
    • Transfer the pizza to the oven (preferably on a preheated pizza stone or a baking sheet).
    • Bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  6. Add Basil and Oil:
    • Once the pizza is out of the oven, add fresh basil leaves on top.
    • Drizzle with a bit of olive oil and sprinkle with salt.
  7. Serve:
    • You can also sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese for extra flavor before serving.

Enjoy your Margherita pizza!
